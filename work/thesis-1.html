Week 1: 08/30 - Inspirations
My inspirations or interests have always been around the the impact of technology on society. Major domains that I have worked with are machine learning, web and mobile development. This summer I was using a front-end web frameworks called Vue.js, which I happened to find later that it was developed by a Parsons allumnus called Evan You. In the tech spectrum it is not just an ordinary framework, Wired put it best in its article called "THE SOLO JAVASCRIPT DEVELOPER CHALLENGING GOOGLE AND FACEBOOK" that Evan and his small team is actually breaking the monopoly of tech giants in the freedom of web.

This really inspired me not only because Evan was a graduate of DT but how a small team of "half designer and half engineer", as how Evan called himself, could drastically shaped the horizon of tech. We have long taken it granted that all advances in tech will come from the mammoths in the tech industry like Google, Facebook and Microsoft etc. However, a small group of creative individuals can still make such a huge impact how we use technology. The world is going through the so-called 4th industrial revolution with the development of machine learning and artificial intelligence. It makes me wonder, what is the next breakthrough that can kind of restore of past active community of individual app developers and dreamers, instead of being constrained in the big company thinkings.

Week 3: 09/13 - 2-Week Timeline
I think it is time to redefine my scope and narrow down my thesis topic to be within an specific domain. The question I always have in mind for years is how we organize our professional networks online. I go to professional conferences and events often and one of the most important aspect of these kind of professional events is the various interaction with speakers and peers. It is essential for any person to cultivate their professional network and get to know their community better. However there is no effective product right now to really reach out to people during events while not make it awkward to keep those contacts. LinkedIn is not event-focused and Facebook Events requires giving out our personal data online.

Week 3: 09/18 - Audience Assignment
It is quite clear from the start that this product will be used by professional event-goers the most. These are the people with regular user needs to either keep up with the industry or get to know their peers at the professional events. On the peripheral, this product can also be used to manage and communicate with personal events attendents. Professional event-goers and personal event-goers share a lot of similarities: they all consist of people who you will develop deeper, longer-going and personal connections later; and people who are just of one-sight memories simply because of the spatial and temporal compulsion withint the events.

Week 6: 10/04 - Prototype
In-Class Prototype
For this week's in-class prototyping session, I designed a set of personas/senarios and ask people to interact in 3 senarios. The first one is an age 18-25 years old college student who is in school and ready to embark on a carrer after graduation. The second one is an age 20-35 years old professional, who is already in the workplace and in the junior-senior level positions within the company. Last one is a successful influcencer who already has a huge group of audience and followers and ready to expand his/her empire. The goal of my prototype is to examine into what type of events these three groups of people are mostly interested in, most willing to interact with people and the purpose of establishing relationships and communications. Within a certain type of events, what event information people are mostly concerned with and how different people reach out for contacts. A detailed prototype test can be seen in the link below.

Design Values
Connectivity with constraints
It could be in fact more encouraging for people to connect with each other if there is clear line of constraints. How long our connected session is going to last is an important issue when people first establish relationships. By recognizing the constraints first, it opened up more possibilities for people to reach out to each other without the hindersight of building a relationship too fast or too early.

Privacy within relationships
Within a relationship, there are various kinds of rapport level. Different rapport level therefore requires different modes of communications. It is important to recognize that privacy cannot be given up instantly when a relationship is eatablished. With an increasing level of rapport, we can finally loosen up the emphasis on privacy but not entirely given away.

Purposeful communication with efficiency
Communication with purpose can save a tremendous amount of time compared to our social norm of communication followed after introducted. By placing job titles, intent before a communication connection, not only it gives up a choice to either connect or not, but we can jump right into the contents without having to communication about backgroud first.

Week 7: 10/16 - Prototype Playtest
Goal
1. To test out a tree-like structure for presenting event information
2. How likely is the user willing to use an in-app chat feature and for what kind of motives

Assumptions
1. The tree-like structure is a simple balanced way for both event organizer to scaffold event information and attendants to navigate
2. People would enjoy an in-app experience when try to contact people
Knowing that my audience is DT, I have to be aware that all testers are familiar with digital interfaces and aware of other event & chat platforms, so that it is comparably easier for them to adopt new applications and navigate through new user experience

Introduction
Design Question
1. How to create a quick customizable event managing tool for event organizers
2. How to enable event attendees to quickly find out information about their peers and speakers.
3. How to connect people better by increasing the bandwidth of information exchanged at a short period of time.

Rules
1. Imagine you’re attending a professional event. which event/session features would you actually use. How likely would you wanna to contact the speaker/peer.
2. Imagine you’re attending a school event (Dorkshop - Javascript), which event/session features would you actually use. How likely would you wanna to contact the speaker/peer.
3. Imagine you’re attending a personal event (Friend Birthday Party), which event/session features would you actually use. How likely would you wanna to contact the speaker/peer.

Week 13: 11/27 - External Playtest
Updated Prototypes
From the feedbacks from last weeks' critic sessions, I decided to shift my thesis to focus more on the ecosystem within a large conference.

An Ecosystem within a large conference
People come to professional conferences for various reasons and are of various personalities. The transparency of information within an conferene - the ability to visualize the information flowing within an conference - is crucial to the chemstry happens at conferences. By laying out the information of people and subsessions needed for the attendents, it is much easier for them to make choices and quickly form sub-groups. The transparency also facilitates collaboration within existed teams as well, as all team members activities can be visualized with the help of the application.

Event utilities, group communications, and archives
In order to further facilicate a holistic user experience within large conferences, event utilities and other functionalities have to be included within the application. Event session informations, group news feed and a channel for members to freely share document can be used to share information and keep a persistent event archive after the event.
